# Power Law / Heavy-Tailed Distributions

**Appears in:** Network Science, Behavioral Economics, Evolutionary Biology, System Dynamics

## Core Concept

Many real-world distributions have heavy tails where a few instances account for the vast majority of the total (80/20 rule, winner-take-all). Most nodes have few connections while rare hubs have millions. Most wealth is held by few people. Most species are rare while few are common. This violates our intuition that expects normal distributions.

## When to Use This Tool

Use power-law thinking when:
- Assuming normal distributions where they don't apply (causes catastrophic prediction errors)
- Systems exhibit preferential attachment or "rich get richer" dynamics
- Planning for tail risks and extreme events
- Allocating attention or resources (focus on vital few, not trivial many)
- Understanding network vulnerabilities (hub networks fail differently than homogeneous networks)

Power laws fundamentally change strategy - from "optimize the average" to "focus on outliers and tail events."
