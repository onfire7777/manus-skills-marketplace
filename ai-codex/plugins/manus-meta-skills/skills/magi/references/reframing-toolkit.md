# Reframing Toolkit (absorbed from Refract)

3-axis perspective rotation methodology for enriching Magi deliberations. Previously a standalone agent (Refract), now integrated as a Magi pre-deliberation tool.

---

## When to Apply

Use before Magi deliberation when:
- The problem feels "stuck" in one frame
- Logos/Pathos/Sophia all evaluate from the same assumptions
- Blind spots may exist that the 3 perspectives won't catch alone
- Complex strategic decisions with multiple stakeholders

---

## 3 Rotation Axes

| Axis | Name | Question | Transforms |
|------|------|----------|------------|
| **視野** | Field / Scope | どのスケールで見るか？ | Zoom Out · Zoom In · Cross-Domain · Temporal Zoom |
| **視座** | Standpoint / Vantage | 誰の立場から見るか？ | Role Shift · Hierarchy Shift · Timeline Shift · Adversarial Shift |
| **視点** | Angle / Lens | どのフレームで見るか？ | Systems Lens · Inversion Lens · First Principles · Narrative Lens |

## Quick Reframing Process

### Step 1: DECODE — Identify the current frame
- What is the dominant metaphor? ("cost problem" / "tech problem" / "people problem")
- What assumptions are implicit?
- Whose perspective dominates?

### Step 2: ROTATE — Apply at least one transform per axis

**Field rotation examples:**
- Zoom Out: "How does this look at the organization level?"
- Zoom In: "What happens to one specific user?"
- Cross-Domain: "How does [unrelated industry] solve similar problems?"

**Standpoint rotation examples:**
- Role Shift: "How does the intern / CEO / customer see this?"
- Adversarial: "If I were the competitor, how would I exploit this?"
- Timeline: "How would we judge this decision in 5 years?"

**Lens rotation examples:**
- Systems: "What feedback loops exist? Where are leverage points?"
- Inversion: "What if we do the exact opposite?"
- First Principles: "Strip away assumptions — what remains?"

### Step 3: ILLUMINATE — Surface what was hidden
- Per rotation: "What's newly visible?" + "What was hiding?"
- Flag any cognitive biases detected

### Step 4: Feed into Magi Deliberation
- Provide reframed perspectives as additional input to Logos/Pathos/Sophia
- Each perspective considers the blind spots identified

## Integration with Magi Deliberation

```
Problem → Reframing (this toolkit) → Enriched problem statement
                                           ↓
                                    Magi Deliberation
                                    ├── Logos (+ blind spots)
                                    ├── Pathos (+ stakeholder views)
                                    └── Sophia (+ strategic reframes)
                                           ↓
                                    Verdict (better informed)
```

## Common Cognitive Biases to Check

| Bias | Sign | Reframing Counter |
|------|------|------------------|
| Anchoring | First option dominates | Inversion lens |
| Sunk cost | "We've already invested..." | Timeline shift (future-only view) |
| Availability | Recent events overweighted | Cross-domain rotation |
| Confirmation | Only supporting evidence cited | Adversarial standpoint |
| Status quo | "It's always been this way" | First principles lens |
| Groupthink | No dissent in discussion | Role shift to outsider |
